Safety Tips

Before You Travel

  • Research your destination's safety and security situation.
  • Check travel advisories and alerts issued by your government.
  • Make copies of important documents like passports, visas, and travel insurance.
  • Share your travel itinerary with a friend or family member.

During Your Trip

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings at all times.
  • Keep your belongings secure and avoid displaying valuable items.
  • Use reputable transportation options and licensed guides.
  • Stay in well-known and safe accommodation options.
  • Follow local laws, customs, and regulations.

Personal Safety

  • Trust your instincts; if a situation feels unsafe, remove yourself from it.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep emergency contact numbers saved in your phone.
  • Use a money belt or concealed pouch for carrying valuables.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information with strangers.

Health and Hygiene

  • Stay updated on required vaccinations and health precautions for your destination.
  • Carry a basic first aid kit with essentials like bandaids, pain relievers, and any necessary medications.
  • Drink bottled water and be cautious of food hygiene practices.
  • Protect yourself from insects by using repellents and wearing appropriate clothing.

Emergency Situations

  • Know the location of the nearest embassy or consulate of your country.
  • In case of emergency, contact local authorities and your embassy/consulate.
  • Follow advice from local authorities during natural disasters or other emergencies.
